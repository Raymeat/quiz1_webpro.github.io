KERAK TELOR

Kerak Telor is one of the most iconic traditional foods from Jakarta, especially associated with the Betawi people. It is a savory snack made from glutinous rice cooked with eggs, either duck or chicken, and mixed with grated coconut that has been toasted beforehand. The mixture is cooked over a charcoal fire, giving it a smoky aroma and crispy texture. Once it is cooked, Kerak Telor is topped with fried shallots and a sprinkling of dried shrimp, creating a unique blend of flavors — savory, slightly sweet, and aromatic. Unlike many other Indonesian dishes, Kerak Telor is dry rather than saucy, which makes it distinct. Traditionally, Kerak Telor is often sold at cultural festivals, street food fairs, or during Jakarta’s anniversary celebrations. The way the vendors cook it in small pans over charcoal, sometimes flipping it without utensils, also adds to the cultural charm of the dish.
